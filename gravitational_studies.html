Gravitational Studies

Exploring the fundamental force that governs the cosmos.

What is Gravity?

Gravity is a natural phenomenon by which all things with mass or energy are attracted to one another. It is one of the four fundamental forces of nature.

Key Concepts in Gravitational Studies

  • Newton's Law of Gravitation: Describes the gravitational force between two masses.
  • Einstein's Theory of General Relativity: Explains gravity as the curvature of spacetime caused by mass and energy.
  • Gravitational Waves: Ripples in spacetime generated by massive cosmic events.

Applications of Gravitational Studies

  • Understanding black holes and neutron stars.
  • Investigating the dynamics of galaxies and the universe's expansion.
  • Developing technologies for satellite navigation and astrophysical observation.

Fascinating Discoveries

  • Detection of gravitational waves by LIGO and Virgo observatories.
  • Insights into the formation and behavior of black holes.
  • Advancements in cosmology through gravitational lensing.
