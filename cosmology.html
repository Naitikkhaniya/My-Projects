Explore Cosmology

Understanding the origins and fate of the universe.

What is Cosmology?

Cosmology is the study of the universe's origins, structure, evolution, and ultimate fate. It combines physics, astronomy, and mathematics to explore phenomena such as the Big Bang, dark matter, and dark energy.

Key Concepts in Cosmology

  • Big Bang Theory: The prevailing model explaining the universe's birth.
  • Dark Matter: Unseen matter that exerts gravitational effects.
  • Dark Energy: The force driving the universe's accelerated expansion.
  • Cosmic Inflation: Rapid expansion immediately after the Big Bang.

Tools Used in Cosmology

  • Telescopes: Observing cosmic phenomena across the electromagnetic spectrum.
  • Simulations: Computational models to replicate universe dynamics.
  • Particle Accelerators: Studying fundamental particles to understand the early universe.

Why Study Cosmology?

Cosmology provides profound insights into the nature of existence, challenging our understanding of time, space, and matter.
