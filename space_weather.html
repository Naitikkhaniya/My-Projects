Space Weather

Understanding the effects of solar activity on Earth and beyond.

What is Space Weather?

Space weather refers to the environmental conditions in space influenced by the Sun's activity, including solar winds, magnetic fields, and energetic particles.

Key Components of Space Weather

  • Solar Flares: Sudden eruptions of radiation from the Sun.
  • Coronal Mass Ejections (CMEs): Massive bursts of solar wind and magnetic fields.
  • Geomagnetic Storms: Disturbances in Earth's magnetic field caused by solar activity.

Effects of Space Weather

  • Disruptions to satellite and communication systems.
  • Power grid failures due to geomagnetic storms.
  • Increased radiation exposure for astronauts and high-altitude flights.
  • Auroras caused by charged particles interacting with Earth's atmosphere.

Monitoring and Forecasting Space Weather

Organizations like NASA and NOAA actively monitor space weather using satellites and ground-based observatories to predict and mitigate its effects.
